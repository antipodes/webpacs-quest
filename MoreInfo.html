Material Type
Book Monographic nonmanuscript language material, microforms of monographic nonmanuscript language material, and essentially textual monographic electronic resources (e.g., books, pamphlets, technical reports).
Journal Serial nonmanuscript language material, microforms of serial nonmanuscript language material, and essentially textual serial electronic resources (e.g., periodicals, magazines, newspapers).
Video/Film Media intended for projection (e.g., filmstrips, motion pictures, slides, videorecordings).
Music Score Printed music material, microform and electronic notated music (e.g., full score, choirs score, books of musical studies and exercises).
Manuscript Score Manuscript music, microforms of manuscript music, and score theses.
Music Recording Musical sound recording (e.g., orchestral performances).
Spoken Recording Nonmusical sound recording (e.g., spoken word, aerobic workout exercise recordings over musical accompaniment.)
Electronic Resource Computer file (e.g., computer software (including programs, games, and fonts), numeric data, computer-oriented multimedia, online systems or services). Important note: do not select this material type if the significant aspect of the content of the item is any other type (e.g., language material, sound recording, videorecording); select that type instead.
Cartographic Material Nonmanuscript maps, globes, atlases, aeronautical charts, navigational charts, celestial charts, remote-sensing images, computer-generated maps, and microforms of nonmanuscript cartographic material.
Manuscript Map Manuscript cartographic materials, microforms of manuscript cartographic materials, and map theses.
2-D Graphic Two-dimensional nonprojectable graphic (e.g., cards, charts, drawings, posters, reproductions of any of these).
Kit Mixtures of various components issued as a unit and intended primarily for instructional purposes. No one component is identifiable as the predominant component (e.g., set of K-12 social studies curriculum material, package of educational test materials).
Mixed Material Materials in two or more forms that are usually related by virtue of their having been accumulated by or about a person or body. The intended primary purpose is other than for instructional purposes (i.e., unlike "Kit"). This category includes archival fonds and manuscript collections of mixed forms of materials, such as text, photographs, and sound recordings.
3-D Object Three-dimensional artifact or naturally occurring object (e.g., models, games, puzzles, sculptures).
Manuscript Material Handwritten or unpublished "single-instance" items (e.g., handwritten or typed letter, galley proof, dissertation).
