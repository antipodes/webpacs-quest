Search Tips
    Examples:
Adjacency The order of words matters. Multiple words are searched together as one phrase.
Note: If your search phrase contains any of these words: AND, OR, AND NOT, BEFORE, AFTER, NEAR, or WITHIN, put quote marks around the phrase, or it will not be searched as a phrase. 		United States supreme court
"war and peace"
Operators

Use AND or OR to specify multiple words in any field and in any order.
Use AND NOT to exclude words.
Parentheses group words together when using Boolean operators.

(annotated bibliography) and child*

(alaska or canada) and (adventure and not vacation)
  Use NEAR to retrieve records that contain the specified words or phrases within ten words of each other in the same indexed field.
Use BEFORE and AFTER to retrieve records that contain the specified words or phrases in a particular order in the record. Any number of words can appear between the specified words or phrases.
Use WITHIN to specify the maximum number of words that may appear between the specified words.		 California near University

chester before arthur

fractal within 3 geometry
  Operators may be used in combination with selections from the operator pulldown menu (as indicated in brackets). History [And]
Naples or Napoli [And]
Borgia or Alexander
Wildcards At least two characters must precede '*' or '**.'
Use '*' to match up to five characters, starting at the specified position in the word, and to truncate from one to five characters.
Use '**' to match any number of characters, starting at the specified position in the word, and for open-ended truncation.
You may also use a question mark ('?') to replace a single character anywhere within a word. 		colo*r
environment* polic*

inter**

Search and Sort
Relevance
  • The relevance of each record is indicated by a display of 1-5 stars.
  • The system ranks the four indexed fields Title, Author, Subject, and Note in that order.
  • For searches on a single word, the system first applies the ranking rule above. It then determines relevance based on one of the following three rules:
    • Most relevant-- The word is one of the first eight words indexed for the same record.
    • Next most relevant-- The word is one of the first thirty-two words indexed for the same record.
    • Last most relevant-- The word is indexed more than once in the same record.
  • For searches including more than one word, the system first applies the ranking rule above. It then determines relevance based on one of the following three rules:
    • Most relevant-- The first two words are very near one another in the index.
    • Next most relevant-- The first two words are near one another in the index.
    • Last most relevant-- The words are indexed more than once in the same record.
